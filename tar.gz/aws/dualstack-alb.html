When you set up a load balancer in AWS, you can use either of these two addresses:

  1. route53-service-alb-1411176768.ap-northeast-1.elb.amazonaws.com
  1. dualstack.route53-service-alb-1411176768.ap-northeast-1.elb.amazonaws.com

Why don't they just support both IPv4 and IPv6 by default?

Well, it's because when IPv6 first came out, not all cloud services needed it. Plus, some users might have specific preferences, so AWS didn't want to force it on everyone.
But hey, as IPv6 becomes more common and starts to feel like "the new normal," there's a chance this whole setup might change.

For now, though, it makes sense to give people options. You know, let them choose what works best for them.
